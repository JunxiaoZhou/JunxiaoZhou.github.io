Work and Study Experience

  • 2025-present I am currently a tenure-track associate professor at Nanjing University, working in Prof. Tao Li's group. My current research focuses on nanophotonics, metasurfaces, optical imaging, edge imaging, quantitative phase imaging, spin-orbit interactions of light, and nonlinear optics. For academic collaboration inquiries, please contact me at junxiaozhou@nju.edu.cn.
  • 2023-2024 I served as a postdoctoral fellow in Din-ping Tsai's group at City University of Hong Kong, where my research focused on nanophotonics, plasmonics, metasurfaces, edge detection, and nonlinear optics.
  • 2019-2023 I completed my PhD training at the University of California, San Diego (UCSD), under the guidance of Zhaowei Liu, and at Hunan University, under the supervision of Shuangchun Wen and Hailu Luo. After my PhD defense, I continued my research as a postdoctoral scholar at UCSD with Zhaowei Liu.

Personal Statement

Dr. Zhou has authored more than 50 peer-reviewed SCI papers, including 30 publications as first author or corresponding author. According to Google Scholar, this body of work has received approximately 3000 citations, with an h-index of 24.

Research by Dr. Zhou has appeared in leading journals such as Nature Reviews Electrical Engineering, Nature Communications, Physical Review Letters, Science Advances, and PNAS. Two publications have been recognized as ESI Highly Cited Papers, and one was selected as a Physical Review Letters Editors' Suggestion. Dr. Zhou has also filed two US invention patent applications and delivered invited talks at international conferences. The research has received coverage from media outlets including Phys.org and AAAS.
